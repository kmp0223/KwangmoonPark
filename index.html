Hi. My name is Kwangmoon Park, and I am a third-year Statistics Ph.D. student at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am currently working on statistical genomics and high dimensional statistics in Professor Keles' research group.

I am mainly interested in questions related to understanding how genes are regulated by distal regions in the genome, particularly by functional non-coding regions.
For that purpose, I develop statistical tools for analyzing High-dimensional genomic data including Hi-C and HiCHIP, and devise methods to link diverse types of genomic data with better statistical interpretation.
Recently, I have started studying incomplete data analysis and causal inference to devise methods for understanding correct relation among gene regulatory elements and protein coding genes.
In the theoretical facet, I enjoy exploring the probabilistic and statistical properties of the methods I develop.

Before joining UW-Madison, I earned a Master's degree in Statistics at the Yonsei University in 2020 and Professor Seung-Ho Kang was my academic advisor. I earned my B.A. in Economics and Statistics at Yonsei University in Korea.
